# Glossary

## A

**Actuator**: A component that converts control signals into physical motion or action.

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**: The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.

**Algorithm**: A process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or other problem-solving operations.

## C

**Calibration**: The process of configuring a sensor or instrument to ensure accurate measurements.

**Computer Vision**: A field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand the visual world.

**Control System**: A system that manages, commands, directs, or regulates the behavior of other devices or systems.

## D

**Deep Learning**: A subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with representation learning.

**Digital Signal**: A signal that represents data as a sequence of discrete values.

**Distributed System**: A system whose components are located on different networked computers.

## E

**Encoder**: A sensor that measures the position or speed of rotation.

**Embedded System**: A computer system that is part of a larger mechanical or electrical system.

**Error**: The difference between the desired and actual values in a control system.

## F

**Feedback**: Information about the result of a process used to modify the process.

**Filter**: A system or device that removes unwanted components or features from a signal.

**Firmware**: Software that provides low-level control for the device's specific hardware.

## H

**Hardware**: The physical components of a computer or other electronic system.

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.

## I

**Intelligent System**: A system that can learn from experience and adapt to new inputs.

**IoT (Internet of Things)**: The network of physical devices embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity.

**Interface**: A shared boundary across which two or more separate components of a computer system exchange information.

## L

**Latency**: The time delay between the cause and effect of some physical change.

**Learning**: The acquisition of knowledge or skills through study, experience, or being taught.

**LiDAR**: Light Detection and Ranging, a remote sensing method using light in the form of a pulsed laser.

## M

**Machine Learning**: An application of artificial intelligence that provides systems the ability to learn and improve from experience.

**Motor Controller**: A device that governs the operation of an electric motor.

**Motion Planning**: The task of breaking down a desired movement task into discrete motions.

## N

**Neural Network**: A series of algorithms that endeavors to recognize underlying relationships in a set of data.

**Noise**: Unwanted disturbances that corrupt signals or measurements.

## P

**Perception**: The ability to sense and understand the environment.

**Physical AI**: Artificial intelligence systems that interact with and operate in the physical world.

**PID Controller**: A control loop feedback mechanism widely used in industrial control systems.

## R

**Real-time**: Describes systems that process data and respond to inputs immediately.

**Robotics**: The intersection of science, engineering, and technology that produces machines called robots.

**Sensor**: A device that detects or measures a physical property and records, indicates, or otherwise responds to it.

## S

**Sensor Fusion**: The combining of sensory data or data derived from disparate sources.

**Signal Processing**: The analysis, interpretation, and manipulation of signals.

**Software**: The programs and other operating information used by a computer.

## T

**Trajectory**: The path that a moving object follows through space as a function of time.

**Training**: The process of teaching a machine learning model using data.

**Trustworthy AI**: Artificial intelligence that is lawful, ethical, and robust.

## V

**Validation**: The process of checking that a model or system performs as expected.

**Vision System**: A system that captures and analyzes visual information from the environment.

## W

**Wireless Communication**: Communication between electronic devices using electromagnetic waves.

**Workflow**: The sequence of industrial, administrative, or other processes through which a piece of work passes.
